Let's go ahead with the step-by-step overview of the **Coronally Advanced Flap (CAF)** technique, since it's one of the mainstays for treating Miller Class I and II recessions with predictable results.

**Here's an outline of the procedure:**

### Step 1: **Preoperative Assessment and Planning**
- Confirm the diagnosis, recession class, and tissue biotype.
- Ensure good plaque control.
- Measure the recession height and width.
- Decide if a connective tissue graft is needed; if so, harvest it in advance.

### Step 2: **Anesthesia and Intrasulcular Incision**
- Administer local anesthesia.
- Make sulcular incisions around the involved teeth, extending interdentally to release the papillae if necessary.

### Step 3: **Design of the Flap**
- Reflect a full-thickness (mucoperiosteal) flap apically beyond the recession margin.
- The extent of apical dissection varies, but typically around 3-4 mm beyond the recession.

### Step 4: **Root Surface Preparation**
- Smooth the root with scaling and planing, remove any calculus.
- Condition the root surface if indicated (but not mandatory).

### Step 5: **Placement of Graft (if used)**
- If a connective tissue graft is used, place it over the recession defect.
- The graft should be thinner at the edges to facilitate adaptation.

### Step 6: **Coronal Advancement**
- Reposition the flap coronally over the graft (or directly, if no graft).
- Secure the flap with sutures to the interproximal papillae or adjacent tissue, ensuring it covers the graft and the recession.

### Step 7: **Postoperative Care**
- Provide periodontal dressing if necessary.
- Advise on oral hygiene to prevent plaque buildup.
- Prescribe analgesics and chlorhexidine rinse.
- Schedule follow-up visits for suture removal and evaluation.
