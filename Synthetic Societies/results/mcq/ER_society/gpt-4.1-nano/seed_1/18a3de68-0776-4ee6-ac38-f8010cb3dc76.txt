### **Iron Therapy: Dosing Schedules & Duration**

#### **1. Dosing Regimens**
- **Standard Oral Iron (Ferrous Sulfate, Gluconate, Fumarate):**
  - **Adults & Children (Over 2 years):** 
    - Typical dose: **100–200 mg elemental iron daily**, divided into 1–2 doses (e.g., 100 mg twice daily).
  - **Pregnant Women:** 
    - Usually **‘elemental iron 60–100 mg/day’**, starting as early as possible.
  - **Malabsorptive or Intolerance Cases:**
    - Use of alternative formulations (e.g., ferric maltol, IV iron if oral is contraindicated).

- **High-Dose Therapy (Severe Anemia):**
  - Short courses of higher doses (e.g., 200 mg elemental iron thrice daily), but monitor for side effects.

---

#### **2. Duration of Therapy**
- **Mild to Moderate Iron-Deficiency Anemia:**
  - **Typically 3–6 months** after hemoglobin normalization to replenish iron stores.
- **Severe Anemia:**
  - 6 months or more, depending on response.
- **Pregnant Women:**
  - Usually throughout pregnancy and postpartum until iron stores are replenished.
- **Monitoring:**
  - hemoglobin should rise by ~1 g/dL per week.
  - Reticulocyte count increases within 3–5 days.
  - Iron indices (serum ferritin, transferrin saturation) monitored to confirm replenishment.

---

### **3. Monitoring Treatment Progress**
- **Frequency:**
  - Hemoglobin: every 2–4 weeks initially.
  - Ferritin and transferrin saturation: every 8–12 weeks.
- **Goals:**
  - Hemoglobin normalization.
  - Restoring ferritin levels >30 ng/mL (indicating iron repletion).

---

### **4. Managing Side Effects**
- **Common Side Effects:**
  - Gastrointestinal upset (nausea, constipation, diarrhea).
  - Discolored stools.
- **Strategies:**
  - Take iron with food if tolerated (though absorption is better on empty stomach).
  - Use stool softeners or dietary fiber for constipation.
  - Split doses or switch formulations if side effects intolerable.
- **Serious Reactions:**
  - Allergic reactions are rare; if occurs, discontinue and consider IV iron.

---

### **5. Special Populations**
- **Pregnant Women:**
  - Preferably oral ferrous sulfate (30–60 mg elemental iron twice daily).
  - Monitor ferritin and hemoglobin every 4–6 weeks.
  - IV iron if oral intolerance or malabsorption.
- **Malabsorption (e.g., Celiac,IBD):**
  - Consider IV iron, especially if oral is ineffective or poorly tolerated.
- **Elderly & Chronic Disease:**
  - Monitor for coexisting deficiencies and contraindications.
