### **Hydrogen Sulfide (H₂S)**

**Toxicity Levels:**
- **Acute Exposure:** 
  - **Low concentrations (less than 10 ppm):** Usually detectable by smell; mild irritation of eyes and respiratory tract.
  - **Moderate (10-50 ppm):** Eye and respiratory irritation, coughing, headache.
  - **High (50-100 ppm):** Severe eye and respiratory tract irritation, nausea, dizziness.
  - **Very high (above 100-300 ppm):** Rapid loss of consciousness, respiratory failure, potentially fatal.
- **Lethal Concentration:** 
  - The OSHA permissible exposure limit (PEL) is 20 ppm (ceiling). 
  - **Immediately dangerous to life or health (IDLH):** 300 ppm.

**Environmental Impact:**
- **H₂S is highly toxic** to aquatic life; even low concentrations can be harmful.
- Contributes to **corrosion** of metals and infrastructure.
- **Odor pollution** at very low levels (as low as 0.0005 ppm) due to its characteristic rotten egg smell.

**Safety Precautions:**
- Use of **gas detection alarms** capable of sensing H₂S at low ppm levels.
- Adequate **ventilation** in areas where H₂S may accumulate.
- Use of **personal protective equipment** (PPE): gas masks or SCBA (self-contained breathing apparatus).
- Implementation of **emergency protocols** to evacuate personnel at high concentrations.

---

### **Methane (CH₄)**

**Toxicity Levels:**
- **Toxicity:** 
  - **Methane is essentially non-toxic** at typical environmental concentrations; it does not pose direct poisoning risks.
  - **Asphyxiation risk** occurs in enclosed spaces where methane displaces oxygen.
  - **Oxygen displacement:** At high concentrations (>5%), methane can reduce oxygen levels, leading to risk of suffocation.
- **Lethal Concentration:** 
  - **Explosive hazard** at concentrations of 5-15% in air.

**Environmental Impact:**
- **Potent greenhouse gas**, with a Global Warming Potential approximately 28-36 times that of CO₂ over 100 years.
- Contributes significantly to **climate change** if released into the atmosphere.
- Does not react readily in the atmosphere but can form ozone at ground level under certain conditions.

**Safety Precautions:**
- **Ventilation** to prevent accumulation in confined spaces.
- Use of **gas detectors** calibrated for methane to identify leaks.
- **Avoid ignition sources**, as methane is highly flammable and explosive when its concentration in air is between 5-15%.
- Regular **leak detection and repairs** in storage and transportation.
