### Rehydration Management in Severe Cholera (High-Output)

**1. Rapid Rehydration:**
- **Initial phase:** Administer **ORT (Oral Rehydration Therapy)** aggressively, typically **100 mL/kg** over the first 3–4 hours.
- For infants and children, the typical volume is around **50–100 mL/kg** during this period.
- For adults, initial rehydration can be **5 liters or more** depending on dehydration severity.

**2. Intravenous Fluids:**
- If the patient cannot tolerate oral fluids or if there is shock, initiate IV fluids immediately.
- Use **Ringer's lactate (Hartmann’s solution)** as the preferred IV solution.
- Start with **20 mL/kg** boluses, repeated as needed to restore perfusion and blood pressure, monitoring closely for signs of fluid overload.

**3. Ongoing Rehydration:**
- After initial stabilization, continue ORT with electrolyte and glucose solutions.
- Adjust volume based on stool output and ongoing losses.
- In high-output cases, stool volumes can be 1–2 liters per hour, necessitating close monitoring and continual repletion.

**4. Monitoring:**
- Check vital signs, urine output, mental status, skin turgor, and capillary refill.
- Monitor serum electrolytes, blood urea nitrogen (BUN), and creatinine if resources permit.
- Watch for signs of overhydration, especially in children and older adults.

**5. Transition to Maintenance Therapy:**
- Once volume status stabilizes, switch to maintenance ORT.
- Continue rehydration until stools subside and patient can tolerate oral intake freely.

- **Antibiotics:** They can reduce stool volume and duration of illness and are recommended in severe cases.
- **Electrolyte management:** Replace potassium and bicarbonate as needed, especially in ongoing diarrhea.
- **Prevent dehydration-related complications** like hypovolemic shock, electrolyte imbalances, and renal failure.
