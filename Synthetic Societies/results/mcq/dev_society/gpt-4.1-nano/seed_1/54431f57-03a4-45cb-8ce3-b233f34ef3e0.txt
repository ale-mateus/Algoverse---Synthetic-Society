### **Quick Tips: Microcolon & Differential Diagnoses in Neonatal Obstruction**

**Causes of Microcolon:**
- **Ileal or jejunal atresia:** Lack of intrauterine passage of meconium results in a small, underdeveloped colon (microcolon).

---

**Key Differential Diagnoses of Neonatal Microcolon:**
- **Intestinal atresias:** Ileal/jejunal atresia (most common), colonic atresia.
- **Meconium ileus:** Due to thick meconium block, commonly in cystic fibrosis.
- **Hirschsprung disease:** Usually shows a dilated segment proximal to a narrow distal colon; microcolon is less typical but possible in very distal Hirschsprung.
- **Functional conditions:** Neonatal ileus with no structural abnormality, less likely to produce microcolon.

A contrast enema showing a small, microcolon with dilated proximal bowel suggests underlying atresia or meconium ileus—prompt surgical evaluation is essential.
