### **Cervical Cerclage: Indications, Timing, and Types**

#### **Indications**
- **Cervical incompetence:** Painless cervical dilation in the second trimester leading to miscarriage or preterm birth.
- **History of second-trimester loss:** Recurrent pregnancy losses associated with painless cervical dilation.
- **Proven cervical insufficiency:** Cervical length <25 mm before 24 weeks, especially with other risk factors.
- **Detected cervical funneling:** On ultrasound during pregnancy.

*Note:* Not indicated for always managing crisis bleeding or spontaneous miscarriage without cervical insufficiency.

---

#### **Timing**
- **Prophylactic cerclage:**  
  - Placed usually between **12-14 weeks** of gestation in women with history indicating cervical incompetence (e.g., previous second-trimester losses).
- **Ultrasound-guided cerclage:**  
  - For cervical shortening or funneling detected during pregnancy, inserted ideally before dilation progresses.
- **Emergent cerclage:**  
  - Placed in the presence of painless cervical dilation in the second trimester, often called "rescue cerclage," to prolong pregnancy.

---

#### **Types of Cerclage**
- **McDonald Cerclage:**  
  - The most common.  
  - A simple purse-string suture placed around the cervix at the level of the internal os via vaginal approach.
- **Shirodkar Cerclage:**  
  - More complex, involves dissecting and suturing at a higher level closer to the internal os, often done in selected cases.
- **Laparoscopic or abdominal cerclage:**  
  - Performed via laparotomy or laparoscopy, indicated when vaginal cerclage has failed or isn't feasible.
