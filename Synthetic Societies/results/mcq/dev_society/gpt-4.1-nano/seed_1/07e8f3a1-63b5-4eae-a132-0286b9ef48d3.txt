- **Clinical Relevance:** Wrinkle formation around the eyes; Bell's palsy often affects this region, leading to inability to close the eyelid tightly.

#### **3. Buccal Branches**
- **Supply:** Muscles of the upper lip, nose, and parts of the mouth like buccinator, orbicularis oris
- **Clinical Relevance:** Exercise of facial expressions involving the mouth; important in smiling, puckering, and speech articulation.

#### **4. Marginal Mandibular Branch**
- **Supply:** Muscles of the lower lip and chin (e.g., depressor anguli oris, depressor labii inferioris, mentalis)
- **Clinical Relevance:** Lower lip movement; injury causes an asymmetrical smile or drooping of the lower lip.

#### **5. Cervical Branch**
- **Supply:** Platysma muscle
- **Clinical Relevance:** Expression of tension in the neck; paralysis causes loss of neck tension and asymmetrical expressions.

---

### **Additional Notes:**

- **Injury to these branches** (e.g., due to surgery or trauma) leads to facial weakness or paralysis, depending on the site and nerves involved.
